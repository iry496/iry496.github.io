Profile

Trustworthy models, evidence you can act on.

I am an applied quantitative researcher and educator working on trustworthy machine learning, reproducible data science, and decision-oriented analytics. I am currently a Doctor of Technology (D.Tech.) student at Purdue University, with an A.L.M. in Data Science from Harvard University Extension School and graduate study in financial engineering.

My current work emphasizes leakage-aware validation, secure data workflows, and evidence evaluation in biomedical data science — from biomarker and therapeutic-target claim assessment to reproducible evidence dossiers — with risk modeling as a supporting methodology for calibration, uncertainty, and practical decision support.

Alongside research, I have taught at California State University, Los Angeles since 2012, spanning statistics, data science, and information security. I care about work that is transparent, rigorous, and genuinely useful.
